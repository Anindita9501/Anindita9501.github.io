About

"Bridging theory with real-world application — one student, one system, one interaction at a time."

I'm a recent Computer Science graduate from BRAC University with a deep interest in Human-Computer Interaction, usability research, and cybersecurity. My undergraduate thesis explored the usability of Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) wallets on feature phones — a space where security and accessibility must coexist for underserved communities.

Beyond research, I've spent over a year as a Teaching Assistant at BRAC University, helping students navigate 100–200 level CS courses. I care about making computing education accessible, clear, and engaging.

I'm also a curious builder — I've worked on everything from blockchain DApps to assembly language games to Arduino-based accident detection systems.
